Sleep quality, sleep disturbances, and sleeplessness

The Project

Background:

Poor quality sleep is common in Parkinson’s; and can begin many years before motor symptoms are first noticed. In otherwise healthy people, it has been established that not getting enough sleep and/or broken sleep causes problems with learning, memory, and decision making. People with Parkinson’s often experience these same problems, but there has been little exploration of their connection with sleep quality. This is a problem that PhD candidate Maria Pushpanathan hopes to fix.

Methods:

Participants completed a comprehensive neuropsychological testing battery, aimed at identifying problems with different areas of thinking and memory. After this, participants completed a single night sleep study in their homes. The sleep study is a specialised method of monitoring an individual's physiological processes while they are asleep, collecting data on brain waves, eye movements, and oxygen levels. This approach will show:

  • Whether people with Parkinson's have different quality sleep than control participants
  • The physiological mechanisms behind these differences
  • Whether individuals with poorer sleep had more cognitive problems, and
  • Whether this was the same for people with Parkinson's

Progress:

Maria is now in the final stages of writing her thesis. Her first results chapter has been published in Sleep Medicine Reviews. The abstract is available here:
‘The Relationship Between Sleep and Cognition in Parkinson’s Disease: A Meta-Analysis’
