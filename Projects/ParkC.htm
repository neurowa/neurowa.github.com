The Project

Background:

Parkinson's is the second most common neurological condition in Australia (after dementia). Everyone experiences Parkinson's differently, some people may experience tremor, some rigidity, and some neither. So far, nobody has clearly shown whether this heterogeneity represents 'subtypes' and whether these change differently over time. That's where we come in.

Methods:

To test this, we're assessing individuals on a broad range of measures (motor, cognitive, and psychological) every two years. Taking this approach allows us to look at a few things:

  • Whether individuals' combinations of symptoms clearly fall into groups
  • Whether individuals stay in the same groups over time, and
  • Whether the rate of symptom progression is different in each group

Progress:

The study has now been running for over 8 years, with some individuals now completing their fourth follow-up session. While the primary outcome (subtyping) is yet to be assessed, several smaller research projects have been conducted with the data. We currently have over 250 individuals with Parkinson's in the study, but we are always looking for more volunteers.
