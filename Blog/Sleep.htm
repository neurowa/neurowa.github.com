Sweet Dreams: Benefits of Sleep in Parkinson's <- Back to the blog

New research by the lab's own Maria Pushpanathan is taking the Parkinson's community by storm. As part of her PhD, Maria conducted a comprehensive statistical examination of all available research into the relationship between sleep and cognition in Parkinson's. Maria identified that an individuals quality of sleep at night had a significant impact on their global cognitive functioning and memory.

Almost 98% of people with Parkinson's experience some form of sleep disturbance, and Maria hopes that her research will bring some awareness to the importance of helping these individuals get a good night's sleep.

“Approximately 70, 000 Australians are living with Parkinson’s - imagine how life would be different if sleep was no longer a struggle, and memory loss could be delayed, reduced, or even prevented.."Maria Pushpanathan

The full study was published in Sleep Medicine Reviews, and can be accessed here. For more information about Maria's research head on over to her project page.
